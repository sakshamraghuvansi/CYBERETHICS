Plagiarism

Plagiarism is the act of using someone else's work, ideas, words, images, or any creative content without giving proper credit to the original creator and presenting it as your own. It is considered a violation of intellectual property rights and is unethical as well as punishable in academic and professional fields. Plagiarism can occur when content is copied from books, research papers, websites, assignments, or even AI-generated text without acknowledgment. It reduces originality, discourages creativity, and harms the credibility of the person involved. To avoid plagiarism, one should always cite sources, paraphrase properly, use quotation marks for copied text, and create original content.

S.No Types Defination
1. Direct Plagiarism Copying text word-for-word from a source without citation.
2. Mosaic / Patchwork Plagiarism Mixing copied phrases from multiple sources and presenting them as original.
3. Complete Plagiarism Submitting someone else's entire work as your own.
