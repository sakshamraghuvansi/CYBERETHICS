Cyberbullying


Cyberbullying is a form of harassment or bullying that takes place through digital devices such as smartphones, computers, and tablets using platforms like social media, messaging apps, online games, forums, or emails. It includes actions like sending abusive messages, spreading rumors, sharing embarrassing photos or videos, threatening someone online, or intentionally hurting someone through digital means. Unlike traditional bullying, cyberbullying can happen anytime, anywhere, and messages or media spread quickly to a large audience, making it more harmful and difficult to control. Cyberbullying deeply affects a persons emotional and mental well-being, leading to stress, anxiety, low self-esteem, social withdrawal, and in severe cases, depression or self-harm. To prevent cyberbullying, users must behave respectfully online, think before posting, avoid sharing personal information publicly, and report or block abusive accounts. Schools, parents, and society should spread awareness, encourage positive digital behavior, and support victims so that the internet remains a safe and healthy space for everyone.
