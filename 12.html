Online harrasment

Online Harassment refers to intentional and repeated behavior conducted through digital platforms to threaten, insult, abuse, or intimidate a person. It includes harmful actions such as sending offensive messages, spreading rumors, stalking, or sharing private content without consent. It is a form of cybercrime and seriously affects a person mental and emotional well-being.

Types of online harrasment-

Cyberbullying:
Harassing or humiliating someone on social media, chats, or games.
Trolling:
Posting provoking or insulting comments to create conflict.
Cyberstalking:
Continuous monitoring, threatening, or tracking someone's online activities.
Doxxing:
Publicly sharing someone private information without permission.
Impersonation:
Creating fake accounts to defame or deceive others.
Outing/Non-consensual sharing:
Sharing someone private photos, texts, or information online without consent.
Mobbing:
A group harassing or targeting one person online.
