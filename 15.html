Misinformation and fake-news
Misinformation refers to false or inaccurate information spread without the intention to harm.
Fake News is deliberately created and shared to mislead people, influence opinions, or create confusion.
Impact How to spot it?
Creates panic, fear, and confusion among people Verify the information from multiple trusted news sources
Causes social conflict and hatred between communities Notice if the content is emotionally charged or sensational
Economic losses through frauds and misleading claims Analyze website URL, spelling errors, fake-looking designs
Manipulates beliefs and opinions Check date/context — outdated news may be reshared
Fake news creates panic, spreads hatred, harms democracy, and misguides society. It can be spotted by checking sources, verifying facts, confirming through reliable media, cross-checking dates, analyzing biases, and using fact-checking tools before sharing.
